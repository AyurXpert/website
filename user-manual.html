System Overview

AyurXpert is a multi-tenant, cloud-based Hospital Management System built specifically for the Ayurveda ecosystem — clinics, hospitals, Panchakarma centres, teaching institutions, and dispensaries. Every organisation (called a tenant) gets its own isolated data, branding, and staff, all on the same shared platform.

Roles & Access

Every staff member has exactly one role. The role controls which pages they can open and what they can do. A super_admin can access every page in the system.

Super Admin
→ admin.html
Full access to all modules. Approves staff and fees. Typically the owner or IT manager.
Dept Admin
→ admin.html
Department-level management. Can approve fees at dept level, manage OPDs and IPD setup.
Doctor
→ doctor.html
Views patient queue, records consultation notes, writes prescriptions, requests IPD admission.
Receptionist
→ reception.html
Registers patients, creates visits, collects fees, enrolls ABHA, manages live queue.
Pharmacist
→ dispensaryPOS.html
Dispenses prescriptions, manages inventory, handles GRN and purchase orders.
Nurse
→ reception.html
Same access as receptionist. Can also view doctor queue to assist patients.
Therapist
→ therapist.html
Views and manages their own Panchakarma therapy sessions for the day.
Accountant
→ fee-admin.html
Creates fee structures (pending approval), views billing reports and collections.
Lab Tech
→ reports.html
Access to lab-related reports (placeholder for future lab module).
Note: A staff member's role is assigned when they join via the signup flow and is approved by the super_admin or dept_admin before they can log in.

1. Register Your Organisation

Done once by the owner/super_admin. Opens register.html — no login required.

Go to register.html
Fill in organisation name, type (clinic / hospital / PK centre / dispensary / college), city, state, owner full name, email, and password.
Submit the form
AyurXpert creates your organisation record and assigns you the super_admin role automatically.
Note your Tenant Code
After registration, your unique Tenant Code appears (e.g. SRST-4821). Share this code with all staff so they can join your organisation.
Log in
Go to login.html, enter your email → your name and role appear → enter password. You land on admin.html.
2. Add Staff Members
Staff Sign Up (signup.html)

Each staff member does this on their own device. They do not need a pre-created account.

Open signup.html
Enter their full name, phone, email, password, and the Tenant Code given by the admin.
Select role
Choose from the role list: doctor, receptionist, pharmacist, nurse, therapist, lab_tech, accountant, etc.
Submit
Account is created with status pending_approval. They cannot log in until approved.
Approve Staff (admin.html)

Super admin or dept_admin approves pending staff from the Dashboard.

Open admin.html
The "Pending Approvals" section shows all staff awaiting approval with their name, role, and phone.
Click Approve or Reject
Approved staff status changes to active and they can log in immediately.
Tip: Doctors need to have their qualification and registration number set in their profile (super_admin can edit this) — these print on prescriptions.
3. Configure OPDs

Page: opd-admin.html  |  Access: super_admin, dept_admin

Seed NCISM OPDs (Teaching Hospitals)
Click "Seed NCISM OPDs" (gold button, top right)
AyurXpert checks which of the 10 mandatory NCISM OPDs are missing and shows a confirmation list. Click OK to create them all at once.
Verify the compliance banner disappears
Once all 10 are active, the amber NCISM compliance warning banner goes away.
NCISM Mandatory OPDs (10): Kayachikitsa · Panchakarma · Shalya Tantra · Shalakya Netra Roga · Shalakya KNM · Stri Roga & Prasuti Tantra · Kaumarabhritya · Swasthavritta & Yoga · Screening OPD · Agada Tantra
Add a Custom OPD
Click "+ New OPD"
An inline form opens. Enter the OPD name and optionally select an NCISM code from the dropdown (it auto-fills the name and description).
Save OPD
The OPD appears in the left list with a green Active badge (or a gold NCISM badge if coded).
Assign Doctors to an OPD
Click an OPD from the left list
The right panel loads the doctors currently assigned to that OPD.
Use "Add a doctor" dropdown
Select a doctor from the dropdown (shows all active doctors not yet assigned) and click Add. The doctor is added and marked active for today.
Toggle "Active Today"
Each morning, toggle which doctors are present. Only active-today doctors appear in the Reception OPD dropdown.
4. Fee Structures

Page: fee-admin.html  |  Access: super_admin, dept_admin, accountant

Fees have a 3-level approval workflow: Accountant creates → Dept Admin approves → Super Admin activates.

Create a fee (Accountant / Dept Admin)
Click "+ New Fee". Select OPD, fee type (Registration / Consultation / Procedure / Investigation / Package), enter amount, label. Status = pending.
Dept Admin approval
Dept admin sees pending fees and clicks "Dept Approve". Status → dept_approved.
Super Admin activation
Super admin sees dept-approved fees and clicks "Activate". Status → active. Now this fee appears in Reception when creating a bill.
Shortcut: Super admin can bypass dept approval and directly activate any fee.
5. Medicine Inventory Setup

Page: inventory.html  |  Access: super_admin, dept_admin, pharmacist

Adding a Medicine
Click "+ Add Medicine"
Fill in the medicine name, brand, unit (tablet/ml/gm), category, and classical reference if applicable.
Set pricing
Enter MRP. Set profit% — the system auto-calculates your selling price. Set reorder level and max stock.
Add NAMC indications (optional)
Tag the medicine with NAMASTE codes (e.g. Jvara, Amavata). These connect it to doctor.html classical Rx suggestions.
Upload image (optional)
Upload a product image — stored in Supabase and shown in the dispensary.
CSV Import (Bulk)

Download the CSV template, fill it with your medicine list (name, brand, unit, MRP, stock, etc.), and upload. AyurXpert imports all rows at once.

Stock Adjustment

Click the ± Adjust button on any medicine row to add or subtract stock manually (e.g. after a stocktake). Every adjustment is logged.

Receiving Stock — Purchase (GRN)

Page: purchase.html. When goods arrive from a supplier:

Select supplier, enter invoice number and date
Add line items
Search medicine by name or scan barcode. Enter quantity, batch number, expiry date, purchase rate.
AI Receipt OCR (optional)
Take a photo of the supplier invoice and upload — Claude AI extracts all medicine names, quantities, and rates automatically.
Save GRN
Stock levels update automatically across the system.
6. IPD & Bed Setup

Page: bed-admin.html  |  Access: super_admin, dept_admin

Create Departments
Click "Seed NCISM Depts" (gold button)
For teaching hospitals — creates all 10 mandatory NCISM departments in one click. The NCISM compliance banner shows which are still missing.
Or click "+ New Department"
Fill name, select NCISM code (optional), link to an OPD, and toggle PG status. If PG, enter sanctioned seats — AyurXpert calculates the beds required (seats × 4).
Add Beds
Bulk Add (recommended for new setup)
Click "+ Bulk Add Beds". Select department, enter prefix (e.g. "A"), count (e.g. 20), start number (e.g. 1). Preview shows A-01, A-02… Click Add Beds.
Or add single beds
Click "+ Bed" on a department card. Set bed number, ward, type (General / ICU / Private / PK Treatment), floor, and whether it counts as a PG-allocated bed.
Bed Matrix View

Switch to the Bed Matrix tab to see all beds as colour-coded cards: Green = Vacant, Red = Occupied, Grey = Maintenance, Gold = Reserved. A small blue dot marks PG-allocated beds. Filter by department, status, or bed type.

NCISM Ratio: PG departments need 4 beds per PG seat with ≥80% occupancy. Departments violating this show a red ⚠ Ratio badge on the card.
Reception

Page: reception.html  |  Access: receptionist, nurse, dept_admin, super_admin

This is where every patient's day begins. The receptionist creates a visit, generates a bill, collects payment, and the patient joins the doctor's queue.

Registering a New Patient
Enter phone number
Type the patient's mobile number in the search box. AyurXpert searches for existing patients with that number (up to 6 family members with the same phone can appear).
Select or create patient
If the patient exists, click their name card — all fields auto-fill. If new, enter name, age, gender, date of birth, and blood group.
Select OPD and Doctor
Choose from active OPDs. Doctors shown are only those marked "active today" for that OPD.
Chief complaint
Briefly enter the patient's main complaint (e.g. "Knee pain since 3 months"). This travels with the visit to the doctor.
Bill & payment
Registration fee and consultation fee auto-populate from active fee structures. Add any additional charges. Select payment mode (Cash / UPI / Card / Credit) and click Pay.
Done
Patient gets a token number and appears in the doctor's queue as "Waiting".
Family Members

Multiple patients can share the same phone number (family). When you search a phone, all family members appear as separate name cards. Select the right person — their individual records (age, gender, ABHA) are kept separate.

ABHA Enrollment (National Health ID)

Below the patient form there is an ABHA Enrollment panel. Use this to link the patient's Ayushman Bharat Health Account:

Click "Enroll via Aadhaar OTP"
Enter the patient's Aadhaar number. An OTP is sent to their Aadhaar-linked mobile.
Enter OTP → Verify
After OTP verification, the patient's ABHA number (14-digit) and ABHA address (@sbx) are created and saved to their record.
ABHA Verify Panel

For patients who already have an ABHA, use the Verify panel to pull their demographic data. Four methods are supported:

MethodHow to use
ABHA NumberEnter 14-digit ABHA number → Send OTP → Verify. Demographics auto-fill.
Aadhaar OTPEnter Aadhaar → OTP → Verify. Fastest for returning patients.
Mobile OTPEnter mobile number → OTP from ABDM → Verify.
ABHA AddressEnter @sbx or @abdm address → Verify. (Sandbox limitation: returns empty for @sbx addresses — expected behaviour, shown in FT Report.)
Scan & Share QR

Patients can share their ABHA profile by scanning a QR code (from the ABHA app):

Click "Scan & Share QR"
A QR code appears on screen. The patient opens their ABHA mobile app and scans it.
Auto-fill
Within 2 seconds of the patient scanning, their name, DOB, gender, and ABHA number auto-fill into the registration form via real-time subscription.
Doctor / Clinical Queue

Page: doctor.html  |  Access: doctor, nurse, dept_admin, super_admin

The doctor sees a live list of waiting patients on the left. Clicking a patient opens a full 9-tab clinical documentation panel on the right.

The 9 Clinical Tabs
TabWhat to fill
1. HistoryChief complaint (from reception), history of present illness, past medical history, personal history (diet, lifestyle, habits), family history.
2. VitalsBP, pulse, temperature, weight, height, BMI (auto-calculated), SpO₂, respiratory rate.
3. Ashtasthana Pariksha8-fold Ayurvedic examination: Nadi (pulse), Mutra (urine), Mala (stool), Jihwa (tongue), Shabda (voice), Sparsha (skin), Drik (eyes), Akriti (appearance).
4. Dashavidha Pariksha10-point patient constitution examination: Prakriti, Vikruti, Sara, Samhanana, Pramana, Satmya, Sattva, Ahara Shakti, Vyayama Shakti, Vaya.
5. Systemic ExamCardiovascular, respiratory, GI, CNS, musculoskeletal, and local examination findings.
6. DiagnosisNAMASTE dual-coding: search and select both a NAMC code (Ayurvedic) and an ICD-10 code (international). Both are saved and appear on the prescription.
7. PrescriptionAdd medicines by name (free text or from inventory). Set dosage, frequency, duration. Classical Rx suggestions (green = from your stock, gold = AFI formulations) appear based on the diagnosis NAMC code.
8. AdvicePathya-Apathya (dietary dos and don'ts), yoga, lifestyle advice, follow-up date.
9. DispositionMark visit as complete, refer to another department, or initiate IPD admission.
NAMASTE Dual Coding (Diagnosis Tab)

Type the disease in English or Sanskrit (e.g. "Jvara" or "fever") in the NAMC search box. A dropdown shows matching Ayurvedic terms. Select one. The ICD-10 equivalent auto-suggests — confirm or search for a different one. Both codes are saved together on the consultation record.

Classical Rx Suggestions (Prescription Tab)

Once a NAMC diagnosis is selected, two types of suggestions appear automatically:

  • Green cards — Medicines from your own dispensary inventory tagged with this NAMC code
  • Gold cards — Classical AFI (Ayurvedic Formulary of India) formulations linked to this condition

Click a card to add it directly to the prescription.

Sending to Dispensary

When the prescription is complete, click "Send to Dispensary". This creates a prescription record that immediately appears in the Dispensary POS queue. The visit status changes to "completed".

Dispensary

Page: dispensaryPOS.html  |  Access: pharmacist, dept_admin, super_admin

Prescription queue
All prescriptions sent from doctor.html appear here in real-time with patient name, doctor, and medicines listed.
Click a prescription to dispense
The medicines are shown with current stock levels. If a medicine is out of stock it is flagged in red.
Confirm quantities and dispense
Adjust quantities if partial dispensing. Click "Dispense" — stock levels reduce automatically.
Print invoice
A dispensary invoice is generated and printable from printInvoice.html. Includes medicines, quantities, and amount.
Low stock alerts: Medicines below their reorder level are highlighted. Use purchase-order.html to auto-generate a Purchase Order for all low-stock items grouped by supplier.
IPD Operations

Page: ipd.html  |  Access: super_admin, dept_admin, doctor, receptionist, nurse

Admitting a Patient
Click "+ Admit Patient"
A 2-step drawer opens.
Step 1 — Search patient
Type the patient's phone number or name. Select the correct patient from the results. (Patient must already be registered at Reception.)
Step 2 — Clinical details
Select department → the vacant bed picker loads. Click a bed to select it (green = vacant, PK beds shown in blue). Select admitting doctor. Enter primary diagnosis, diet type, admission date, and notes.
Click "Admit Patient"
The IPD admission is created. The selected bed status changes to Occupied automatically. The patient appears in the admissions table.
Admissions Table

The main table shows all current admissions (default filter) with patient name, bed number, department, doctor, admission date, length of stay, and status. Use the filters to view by department, status (admitted / discharged / LAMA / transferred), or search by patient name.

Discharging a Patient
Click the red ✖ icon
On the admission row in the table. The discharge drawer opens showing admission summary.
Select discharge type
Discharged (regular) · LAMA (Left Against Medical Advice) · Transferred (to another facility) · Deceased. For transfers, a hospital name field appears.
Enter discharge date, condition, and summary
Discharge summary is saved with the admission record for 7-year NABH retention.
Click "Confirm Discharge"
The bed is automatically freed (status → Vacant) and the admission status updates.
Stats Dashboard

The top row shows: Currently Admitted · Vacant Beds · Average Length of Stay (live calculation) · Discharges Today.

Panchakarma Therapy Sessions

Page: therapist.html  |  Access: super_admin, dept_admin, doctor, therapist, nurse

This page manages the daily schedule of Panchakarma therapy sessions for both IPD and OPD patients.

Date Navigator

Use the ‹ › arrows or the date picker to move between days. Click Today to return to the current date. All session counts and the table reload for the selected date.

Scheduling a Session
Click "+ Schedule Session"
The scheduling drawer opens.
Select patient source
IPD Admission — picks from currently admitted inpatients (shows bed number). OPD — for outpatient PK therapy.
Select therapy phase and name
Phase: Purvakarma (prep) / Pradhanakarma (main) / Paschatkarma (post). Therapy name has a 20+ item autocomplete list (Abhyanga, Shirodhara, Basti, Nasya, etc.) or type any custom therapy.
Assign therapist
Therapists are grouped by gender (Male / Female) in the dropdown. The note below shows current M/F count vs NCISM requirement of 2M + 2F per department.
Set date, time, doctor clearance
Tick "Doctor clearance given" if the treating doctor has cleared the patient for this procedure.
Managing Sessions
ActionHow
▶ StartClick the play button — session status → In Progress, start time is recorded.
✓ CompleteClick the check button — opens completion drawer. Enter actual start/end time, therapist notes, confirm doctor clearance.
✗ SkipClick the X button — opens skip drawer. Enter reason (patient unwell, refused, etc.).
📋 View NotesClick the clipboard icon — shows full session details in a read-only drawer.
Therapist Self-View

When a staff member with the therapist role logs in, they see only their own sessions for the day. Doctors and admins see all therapists' sessions.

NCISM Requirement: Each PK department must have 4 therapists (2 Male + 2 Female). The scheduling drawer shows a live count and warns if this ratio is not met.
Reports

Page: reports.html  |  Access: super_admin, dept_admin, accountant

Billing Dashboard (Accountant View)

Shows total collections for a selected date range. Filterable by payment method (Cash / UPI / Card / Credit). Displays individual bill records with patient name, visit details, amount, and payment status.

Date range: Use the From and To date pickers. Click "This Month" or "Today" for quick filters. The total collection updates instantly.
Purchasing Module
Auto Purchase Orders — purchase-order.html

Access: super_admin, dept_admin, pharmacist, accountant

AyurXpert automatically identifies all medicines whose stock has fallen below the reorder level. It groups them by supplier and generates a ready-to-send Purchase Order. Click "Generate PO" → review items → Print or Export CSV to send to the supplier.

Goods Receipt Note — purchase.html

Access: super_admin, dept_admin, pharmacist, accountant

When the ordered goods arrive, create a GRN:

Enter supplier name, invoice number, and date
Add medicines
Search by name or scan barcode. Enter received quantity, batch number, expiry date, purchase rate, and MRP.
AI OCR (optional)
Take a photo of the paper invoice. The AI (Claude Haiku) reads the invoice and extracts all line items automatically — saving manual entry time.
Save GRN
All inventory quantities and batch records update immediately.

Common Issues & Solutions
ProblemSolution
Page shows "Not authorised"Your role doesn't have access. Ask super_admin to check your role assignment.
OPD dropdown empty at ReceptionNo doctors marked "Active Today" in opd-admin.html, or no active OPDs.
Bill fees not showingFee structures not yet active. Go to fee-admin.html and ensure fees are in "active" status.
Medicines not in dispensary queueDoctor hasn't clicked "Send to Dispensary" in doctor.html. Or prescription status is already "dispensed".
Bed picker empty in IPDNo vacant beds in the selected department. Check bed-admin.html → Bed Matrix.
ABHA OTP not receivedABDM sandbox rate limit (3 OTPs per 15 min). Wait 15 minutes and retry.
